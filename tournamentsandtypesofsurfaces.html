Tournaments


The Australian Open is the first grand slam played in the tennis season. The Aussie Open, nickname of Australian Open, is one of the four major tournaments played in the year. Not everyone can compete. In order for you to play in the Open, you must go through the qualifying rounds. The Aussie Open is usually played in January for 2 weeks. The Aussie Open's surface is hard court and is held in Melbourne, Australia.
Roland Garros French Open is the second grand slam played in the tennis season. Usually called Roland Garros or French Open, it's another one of the four major tournaments played in the year. Like any other tournament, you have to go through qualifying rounds. Roland Garros is usually played for the last week of May and first week of June. The surface that is played on in Roland Garros is clay and is held in Paris, France.
Wimbledon is the third grand slam played in the tennis season. Wimbledon's full name is The Championships Wimbledon but called Wimbledon instead and is the third of four major tournaments played in the year. Yes, you still have to go through qualifying rounds. Wimbledon is usually played the last week of June and the first week of July. Wimbledon's surface is called "lawn tennis" and is held in London, Great Britain.
U.S. Open is the fourth and final grand slam played in the tennis season. The U.S. Open doesn't really have a different name to be called unless you want to say "Open". It is the final major tournament of the year. I'll say it one more time, you do need to go into qualifying rounds. The U.S. Open is usually played the last week of August and the first week of September. Open's surface is a hard surface and is held in Flushing, New York.



Types of Surfaces


Hard Surface

Clay Surface

Lawn Tennis Surface


Hard surfaces are made of multiple layers like asphalt, concrete, and covered with acrylic material in order to make the lines of a court for players to play tennis on. There are only two out of the four grand slams that are played on hard surfaces; the U.S. Open and the Australian Open. There are many outside masters that are played on hard surfaces. This surface is the most commonly played on surface.
Clay surfaces are made of either crushed shale, stone, or brick. There are two different types of clay courts; red and green clay. Red is flat and the water is balanced while in green clay, it's slanted for a water run-off. Clay courts are usually common in Europe and Latin America. The French Open is the only one out of the four grand slams that has a clay surface which make it unique.
Lawn tennis surfaces are made of, you guessed it, grass and very packed soil. It's very slippery to play on grass. The ball doesn't bounce above the knee and although the speed is in the ball, the player still has to be quick to play the ball. The only grand slam to have a grass surface is Wimbledon which make it unique to the sport. Lawn tennis is mostly common in England or Europe.
