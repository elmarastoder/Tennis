Tennis Legends

"I never knew I was grunting, it was just part of my strokes."

-Monica Seles

There have been legends in tennis since the first match has been played. Arthur Ashe, Billie Jean King, Steffi Graf, Andre Agassi, Monica Seles, and so much more. If you didn't know about these people, don't worry you're not alone.

Arthur Ashe

Arthur Ashe was the first African-American male to win, and still is, to win the U.S. Open and Wimbledon. He was also the first African-American to be ranked No.1 in th1e world. He was also he first African-American to be placed into the International Tennis Hall of Fame. The U.S. Open even named a court/stadium after Arthur Ashe; Arthur Ashe Stadium. It's the biggest stadium to be played on at the U.S. Open. He also won 8 grand slams during his career.

Billie Jean King

Billie Jean King is one of the first well-known openly gay atheletes. Yes, Billie Jean King is a homosexual and she's not embarassed of it. Billie Jean King has won 12 grand slams. But, she was a Wimbledon champion TWENTY times including mixed doubles, singles, and doubles. She hasn't only won trophies, she had the USTA National Tennis Center named after her; USTA Billie Jean King National Tennis Center. She also fought for equal pay for women tennis players and still does today.

Andre Agassi

Andre Agassi is a former No.1 American tennis player who shocked evrybody with his strong mentality and game. Even though he tested positive for crystal meth use in 1990, people still adored him. He won 8 grand slams. Agassi was one of the fastest tennis players out there. He eventually divorced his wife Brooke Shields and then married Steffi Graf, another great legend.

Steffi Graf

Steffi Graf is one of the great tennis legends out there. Steffi Graf is a former German No.1. She has won 22 grand slams in her career.No woman has been able to as close as tie her record. She married Andre Agassi, a fellow tennis player, and had two kids. Steffi Graf is also known for planning a stabbing on Monica Seles to stay at the top of the charts.



Monica Seles

Monica Seles is a former No.1 tennis player from ex-Yugoslavia. She has won a total of nine grand slam titles. She was the youngest player to win a French Open. She is also an author and speaker. Seles also competed on Dancing With The Stars in 2008. Monica Seles was stabbed in the back during a break in the match. It is said that Steffi Graf planned it to stay a the top of the charts. She is now married to Tom Golisano.

Novak Djokovic

Novak Djokovic from Serbia is currently ranked world's No.1. He has come from a war torn country and has succeded all his dreams. He has currently won 11 grand slams and 29 masters titles which is the most a male tennis player has ever earned Novak Djokovic has won every grand slam; Wimbledon, U.S. Open, Austarlian Open but not the French Open. He is optimistic that he will win the title this year. He is currently married to Jelena Ristic Djokovic and has a son Stefan Djokovic.

Serena Williams

Serena Williams from America is currently ranked No.1 as well. Serena Williams has earned 21 grand slam titles and is currently trying to tie Steffi Graf's record of 22 grand slams. She won her first grand slam when she was only 117 against Matina Hingis. Her sister Venus Williams also has a career in the tennis world. Neither of them have a family. Their father inspired them to play tennis.

Roger Federer

Roger Federer is a Swiss tennis player but a former No.1. He is currently ranked No.3 in the world. Roger Federer has won 17 grand slam titles. Federer was the first Swiss man who won a singles grand slam title which he won in Wimbledon of 2003. He married Mirka Federer in 2009 and then had 4 kids. Mirka Federer was a former tennis player for doubles tennis.



Boris Becker

Boris Becker is a former No.1 tennis player from Germany. Becker won 6 grand slam singles titles. When Becer started as a teenager, he surprised everyone with his powerful serves and moves around the court. In all, Becker won 49 career titles. When he was younger, he practiced with future tennis star Steffi Graf at a Tennis Academy. He married Barbara Becker then Lily Becker and had four kids. Today, he's Novak Djokovic's coach.

Victoria Azarenka

Victoria Azarenka is currently No.5 but a former No.1 from the country of Belarus. Azarenka was an Olympic gold medalist. She is also the first Belarusian woman to win a grand slam singles title. She has only won 2 grand slam titles in her career. Azrenka is known for making grunting noises during a match while hitting the ball. She has dated professional tennis player Sergei Bubka Jr. and seems to have a romantic relation with RedFoo.

Andy Roddick

Andy Roddick is a former American No.1 tennis player who retired in 2012. Although, he has only won one grand slam in his entire career in 2003 at the U.S. Open, he is very well known for his great serves. His speed go at least up to 140 mph. One time in a clay tournament, he played with a fan. The goal was to try and return his serve. THE BALL GOT STUCK IN THE CLAY!!!! He married American model and actress Brooklyn Decker.

Martina Hingis

Martina Hingis is a former No.1 Swiss tennis player. Hingis became the youngest grand slam champion and youngest woman to earn the world's No. 1 ranking. Martina Hingis has won 5 grand slam titles. Despite the injuries she dealt with throughout her career, she still played the sport she loved so much. She lost to Serena Williams in the finals of U.S. Open in 1999 when Serena was only 17 giving Serena her first grand slam title. Hingis is currently married to Thibault Hutin.
